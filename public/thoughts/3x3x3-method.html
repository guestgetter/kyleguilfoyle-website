And here‚Äôs how to answer these questions:

  • What‚Äôs your cost to get an email subscriber?
  • What‚Äôs your target?

To find your ideal target cost per email subscriber (lead), all you need to do is look at how many email leads you‚Äôve received in the last 60 days, and how many sales you‚Äôve made.

This will give you your close ratio, which will help you determine how much you‚Äôll want to spend on a lead.

Example: 200 emails in the last 60 days. And I‚Äôve acquired 7 students. Therefore my close ratio is 3.5%.

For your Roadmapping In Action campaign, you‚Äôll want to at least break even. So we begin by finding the approximate break-even cost per lead.

Let‚Äôs say for every 100 leads you bring in, you should be able to acquire 3 ‚Äì 4 new students.

Let‚Äôs say your training sells for $1,000.

So in theory, 100 leads, could generate $3,000 ‚Äì $4,000 in revenue.

And let‚Äôs go with the low end of $3,000 / 100 leads = $30.00 per lead ‚Üí This is the amount that gives your campaign the best shot of breaking even.

That said, you obviously want to do better than break even. Let‚Äôs say you want to earn a margin of 75%. Then your target cost per lead would be 0.25 x $30.00 = $7.50.

  • What‚Äôs your cost to get a new customer?
  • What‚Äôs your target?
  • What‚Äôs the Lifetime Value of a customer?

If you‚Äôve never run ads before, an easy formula to figure this out is:

Last month‚Äôs marketing spend / # new customers you acquired last month.

So let‚Äôs say you spent $20,000 and 34 new students came into your course.

That‚Äôd be 20,000 / 34 = $588.26 cost per acquisition (CPA).

Similar to your target cost per lead, your target CPA comes down to what your margins are.

(The beauty of courses is that they‚Äôre often very low margin and very scalable).

The question is really: what cost per acquisition would make this campaign a homerun?

There‚Äôs no right or wrong answer ‚Äî it‚Äôs really up to you.

Finally, what‚Äôs the lifetime value of a customer?

This isn‚Äôt an easy number to know ‚Äî it takes time. But as you get clearer on your average customer LTV, you‚Äôll become more confident investing in advertising.

That‚Äôs what this is about.

If you know your customer LTV is $12,000, well you‚Äôll pay $1,000 to get a customer (even if the initial sale is at a loss) because you know that it‚Äôs worth it to get them in your world.

The Simple 3x3x3 Campaign Structure

Step 1: Get Clear On The WHO.

Step 2: Understand what hooks them in. 

Step 3: Get them to tell you what creative direction to take.

Many Facebook advertisers struggle because they have too many options. The 3x3x3 campaign structure is an elegant way to give you constraints that simplify your life.

Your job with the 3x3x3 campaign is to drive as much performance as possible on a relatively ‚Äòlimited canvas.‚Äô

Step 2: 3x Ad Hook Variations: Use These Hooks To Reel ‚ÄòEm In 

Depending on your product or service, your ad copy is what will have the biggest impact on your performance.

And the most important part of your copy is the ad opener, or hook (after all, if you don‚Äôt capture their attention, the rest doesn‚Äôt really matter, does it?)

In the 3x3x3 Method, I put 80% of my copy effort into the hook. Here are 3 powerful frameworks you can use for your Ad Openers:

Dog Whistle Opener: Call out a problem, challenge, pain, or desired outcome / solution that your target market wants.

Like a dog whistle, it‚Äôll only resonate with your ideal students (which is what you want).

Example: Are you a course creator who wants to predictably build Facebook Ads campaigns that can get 1-10 new students in the next 30 days?

Twist The Knife Opener: If you‚Äôre well aware of a pain point that your ideal customer has, then don‚Äôt be afraid to dive right in and twist the knife of that pain.

Example: In 2021, we have more information available to us than ever before YET so many are struggling to actually learn principles that will change their lives. Why is this‚Ä¶?

Objection Opener: If you know the objections that your target audience has to your offer, go right for the jugular and open your copy with an objection.

There are two words that work really well for this: Even if.

Example: Even if you‚Äôve felt nothing but frustration trying to weave your own baskets, I will show you the fun and easy way Basket Weaver Pros 3x their output (in half the time).

Once you‚Äôve got 3 openers, use the following framework for the rest of your ad copy:

  • Stoke their interest by backing up the hook telling them what you have for them.
  • Get them to want what you have by showing them how it will help them.
  • Move them to take action by telling them exactly what to do next.

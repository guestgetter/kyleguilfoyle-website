There Is No Rose of Such Virtue

Traditional 15th-Century English Carol

Arranged for Countertenor and Tenor, a cappella
Anonymous (arr. 2025)

About This Carol

"There Is No Rose of Such Virtue" is one of the most beautiful surviving medieval English carols, dating from the early 15th century. The text comes from the Trinity College Cambridge manuscript and celebrates the Virgin Mary through the metaphor of a rose‚Äîa symbol of purity and divine love in medieval tradition.

This arrangement presents the melody in the countertenor voice, with the tenor providing harmonic support through a simple but rich accompaniment that evokes the sound of medieval polyphony. The setting is designed to be both accessible and beautiful, suitable for intimate performance or contemplative listening.

The arrangement uses modal harmony based on the Dorian mode, giving it an ancient, timeless quality appropriate to the text's medieval origins.

Text

Verse 1 There is no rose of such virtue
As is the rose that bare Jesu;
Alleluia.
Verse 2 For in this rose contained was
Heaven and earth in little space,
Res miranda.
Verse 3 By that rose we may well see
There be one God in persons three,
Pares forma.
Verse 4 The angels sungen the shepherds to:
Gloria in excelsis Deo:
Gaudeamus.
Verse 5 Leave we all this worldly mirth,
And follow we this joyful birth;
Transeamus.
